    Actual indicator available Proportion of population living below the US poverty line.
    Actual indicator available - description Proportion of men, women, and children living in poverty. Only includes people in poverty universe: people not living in institutions and excludes nonrelated children under the age of 15. Disaggregation is provided by sex and age.
    Date of national source publication September 2017
    Graph status notes Graphed
    Graph type description Line graph
    Method of computation Calculated by dividing the number of persons living in households below the poverty line (disaggregated by sex, age and employment status) by the total number of persons (disaggregated by the same sex, age and employment status groups).
    Periodicity Annual
    Scheduled update by national source September 2018
    U.S. method of computation Please see http://www.census.gov/topics/income-poverty/poverty/guidance/poverty-measures.html.
    Indicator variable tot_proportion_belownatpov_line
    Comments and limitations Income questions were redesigned in the 2014 CPS ASEC (which covers 2013). The values shown reflect calculations using the redesigned income questions.
    Date metadata updated October 2017
    Disaggregation geography Age
    Rationale interpretation By combining poverty status with employment status, the concept of the working poor is captured, which aims to measure how many workers, despite being in employment, live in poverty
    Unit of measure Proportion of noninstitutionalized persons in the U.S.
    Disaggregation categories Sex
    Time period 2000-2016
    Scheduled update by SDG team September 2017

    Numéro de l'indicateur 1.2.1
    Nom de la cible By 2030, reduce at least by half the proportion of men, women and children of all ages living in poverty in all its dimensions according to national definitions.
    Numéro cible 1.2
    Description de l'indicateur global This indicator provides the proportion of the total population and the proportion of the employed population living in households with per-capita consumption or income that is below the national poverty line.
